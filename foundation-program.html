The Foundation Program

Inspired by Isaac Asimov’s Foundation series — building leaders in AI and space exploration, with a long-term goal of sending a foster youth to space.

The Foundation Program builds a pipeline for young people who have experienced foster care or other forms of disadvantage. We connect curiosity in AI and space exploration with real skills, mentors, and a path through college into meaningful careers.

Why “Foundation”?

The program takes its name and spirit from Isaac Asimov’s Foundation series — a story about preserving knowledge, building for the long term, and shaping the future through science and leadership. Like Asimov’s Foundation, we are investing in young people today so they can guide tomorrow.

Our north star is ambitious and concrete: we hope to send a foster youth or former foster youth to space. Along the way, we focus on advancing technology — especially artificial intelligence and space exploration — and on making sure that those who have been least likely, historically, to hold power and influence are prepared to play a leadership role in the future.

  • Inspired by Asimov — long-term thinking, scientific progress, and building institutions that outlast any single generation.
  • A foster youth in space — a defining goal that motivates rigorous STEM preparation, mentorship, and career pathways.
  • Advancing technology — hands-on training in AI and emerging fields so participants help shape what comes next.
  • Leadership for the overlooked — equipping young people who have faced foster care and disadvantage to lead in industries and communities that have too often excluded them.

What Participants Receive

  • STEM career exposure — workshops and projects centered on AI, space exploration, and emerging technology fields.
  • Mentorship — one-on-one and group mentoring from professionals in technology, research, and the space exploration industry.
  • Hands-on AI training — practical experience with modern AI tools and agents to learn how to build, problem-solve, and create with AI.
  • College navigation — support applying to strong universities, securing financial aid, and choosing STEM pathways. See our college guide.
  • Post-graduation placement — résumé coaching, internships, and job search support to land roles in AI, tech, and space exploration.

Who It's For

Current and former foster youth and other disadvantaged young people ages 14–24 who are interested in STEM, especially artificial intelligence and space exploration. No prior coding experience required — curiosity and commitment matter most.
